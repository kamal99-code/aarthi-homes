Understanding the 30/360 Day Count Convention

In banking, the 30/360 day count convention is standard for loans and overdraft interest calculation. It assumes each month has exactly 30 days and the year has 360 days. This simplifies accounting because a full month yields exactly 30 days of interest, regardless of its actual length (e.g. February, April, or December).

Algorithm Steps:

  • Date Adjustment: If $Date_1$ starts on the 31st, it is treated as starting on the 30th. If $Date_2$ is the 31st and $Date_1 \ge 30$, $Date_2$ is also treated as the 30th.
  • Days Formula: $\text{Days} = (Y_2 - Y_1) \times 360 + (M_2 - M_1) \times 30 + (D_2 - D_1)$
  • Overdraft Checking: Interest is only accrued during intervals when the running balance is negative (overdrawn debit balance). No interest is calculated on credit/surplus balances.
  • Security Rule: All calculations saved via the app are written to Supabase and Google Sheets. Supabase records automatically purge within 12 hours of submission for privacy.
